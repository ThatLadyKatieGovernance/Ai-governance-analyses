## Overview
NIST AI RMF 1.0 (released Jan 2023) is a **voluntary** framework developed in the US to help organizations manage AI risks. It complements regulation like the EU AI Act.

There is also a **Generative AI Profile** (2024) that addresses risks specific to models like GPT, Grok, etc.

## Core Functions
1. **Govern** – Culture, policies, accountability
2. **Map** – Context and potential impacts
3. **Measure** – Assess risks and impacts
4. **Manage** – Prioritize and act on risks

## Strengths vs EU AI Act
- Flexible and voluntary (easier for companies to adopt)
- Broad applicability (government + private sector)
- Focus on trustworthiness characteristics: valid, reliable, safe, secure, resilient, accountable, transparent, explainable, privacy-enhanced, fair.
