## Overview
The **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)** was released in January 2023. It is a **voluntary** tool designed to help organizations identify, measure, and manage risks of AI systems. 

In 2024, NIST also released the **Generative AI Profile** to address risks specific to large language models and generative systems (like Grok, GPT, Claude, etc.).

**Core Philosophy:** Flexible, adaptable, and organization-wide. It complements regulation rather than replacing it.

## The Four Core Functions

| Function   | Purpose                                      | Key Activities |
|------------|----------------------------------------------|----------------|
| **Govern** | Establish culture, policies, and accountability | AI risk tolerance, roles & responsibilities, culture of risk management |
| **Map**    | Understand context and potential impacts     | Identify intended uses, stakeholders, and foreseeable risks |
| **Measure**| Assess and quantify risks                    | Testing, metrics, evaluation of trustworthiness characteristics |
| **Manage** | Prioritize and respond to risks              | Allocation of resources, treatment plans, monitoring |

## Trustworthiness Characteristics
NIST emphasizes these 8 characteristics organizations should aim for:
- Valid & Reliable
- Safe & Secure
- Resilient
- Accountable & Transparent
- Explainable & Interpretable
- Privacy-Enhanced
- Fair – with Harmful Bias Managed

## Strengths
- Highly practical and flexible for any organization size
- Works well alongside regulations like the EU AI Act
- Strong focus on real-world implementation (not just theory)
- Generative AI Profile is very relevant for frontier models
- Free, publicly available, and widely adopted by U.S. companies and agencies

## Weaknesses & Challenges
- Voluntary nature → limited enforcement power
- Can feel high-level; organizations still need to create their own detailed processes
- Requires significant internal expertise to implement effectively
- Less emphasis on fundamental rights compared to the EU approach
