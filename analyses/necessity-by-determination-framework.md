# Necessity by Determination  
**A High-Risk AI Governance Framework**

**Author:** Katie | [@thatladykatiegovernance](https://github.com/thatladykatiegovernance)  
**Date:** April 24, 2026

### Core Philosophy
High-risk AI systems should only be deployed when **all affected parties collectively determine they are necessary**. This framework emphasizes transparency, education, community self-determination, and prevention of mission creep.

### Required Stakeholders
- Tech Builder  
- Tech Deployer  
- Beneficiaries (law enforcement, data buyers, contractors, etc.)  
- Affected Community Members  
- Independent Verifier  
- Education Representative  

### Pre-Deployment Governance Audit
Before any deployment, all stakeholders must jointly answer:

- **Who** will use it, have access, be affected, benefit, or be harmed?  
- **What** will it be used for?  
- **Why** is it needed?  
- **When & Where** will it be used?

These answers form the system’s **foundational “constitution.”** Any expansion of use cases requires repeating the full audit process.

### Key Safeguards
- Independent verifier reviews all input  
- Red team conducts adversarial testing  
- Whistleblower protections apply  
- Red team members can be replaced annually by vote (emergency exceptions allowed with documentation)  
- **Public disclosure** of the completed governance audit and use cases  
- Unbiased, accessible, ongoing community education including opt-out rights, risk notifications, and feedback mechanisms  

### Guiding Principles
- This framework **works collaboratively with existing laws** (including California’s transparency requirements under AB 2013 and frontier model rules).  
- Public safety remains paramount.  
- In legal gray areas, the high-risk “Necessity by Determination” process must be followed.  

### Comparison to California Law
California currently focuses on developer transparency and targeted impact assessments. My framework strengthens this by requiring **pre-deployment multi-stakeholder approval** and genuine community voice — addressing gaps in public participation and mission creep prevention.

### My Perspective
I created this framework because I see a clear gap in current California policy: while transparency is improving, communities often still lack real decision-making power over high-risk systems that directly affect them. “Necessity by Determination” balances innovation with accountability by giving affected people an educated voice at the table. I believe this collaborative approach can build greater public trust without overly restricting technological progress.

**Goal:** Build sustainable trust through education, transparency, and real community empowerment while responsibly governing high-risk AI systems.
